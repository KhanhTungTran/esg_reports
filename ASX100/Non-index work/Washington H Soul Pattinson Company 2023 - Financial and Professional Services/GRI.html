Any committee member with a personal or professional association with a candidate must disclose that relationship. The Committee member may be required to excuse themselves from the discussion of that candidate; e.f., the Committee will screen the initial list of candidates to identify those candidates who best suit the target profile and conduct interviews with selected candidates; the other commitments of candidates will be considered to ensure they are in a position to commit sufficient time to their Board duties; and g. the results of the interviews will be presented to the Committee, and the Committee will make a recommendation to the Board. The same assessment of the skills required on the Board will be performed when considering a nomination by a shareholder. The Board has approved Guidelines for Dealing with Conflicts of Interest to appropriately manage all perceived, actual and potential conflicts of interest. Directors are required to disclose actual, potential or perceived conflicts and to manage a conflict appropriately; the Director will not receive board papers on the subject of interest but may, at the discretion of the other Directors, be advised that specific board papers have been excluded; cannot be present when the matter is considered unless otherwise permitted by law or the other Directors resolve that the Director in question can be present; cannot vote on the matter unless the other Directors resolve that the Director in question can vote; cannot have access to minutes of the Board or any Board Committee meeting about the subject of interest; and may be required by the Board to take such other steps as are necessary and reasonable to resolve any conflict of interest within an appropriate period.
